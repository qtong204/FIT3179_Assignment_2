ASEAN Environmental Indicators Map (2005–2020)

Environmental Indicators by Country

Regional Environmental Overview

This choropleth map visualizes key environmental indicators across ASEAN nations between 2005 and 2020. Use the selector to toggle between different metrics including CO₂ emissions, renewable energy adoption, and air quality measurements.

The color intensity represents the magnitude of each indicator, with darker shades indicating higher values. This spatial representation helps identify regional patterns and disparities in environmental performance.

Key Insights

  • Singapore shows consistently high CO₂ emissions per capita
  • Laos and Cambodia have made significant renewable energy progress
  • Urban centers generally exhibit higher PM2.5 exposure levels

ASEAN: GDP vs CO₂ Emissions (2010–2020)

Economic Growth vs Carbon Emissions

Economic-Environmental Relationship

This bubble chart explores the correlation between economic output (GDP) and carbon emissions across ASEAN countries from 2010 to 2020. Each bubble represents a country, with size indicating population and color distinguishing different nations.

The general upward trend suggests that economic growth has historically been coupled with increased emissions. However, some nations are beginning to show signs of decoupling as they transition to cleaner energy sources.

Key Insights

  • Indonesia and Thailand show the strongest correlation between GDP growth and emissions
  • Vietnam demonstrates rapid economic growth with moderate emission increases
  • Singapore achieves high GDP with relatively lower emission intensity

Renewable Energy Share Over Time (2010–2020)

Renewable Energy Transition

Renewable Energy Transition

This stacked area chart illustrates the evolution of renewable energy adoption across ASEAN nations from 2010 to 2020. Each colored layer represents a country's contribution to the regional renewable energy mix, normalized to show relative proportions.

The chart highlights how different countries have contributed to the overall growth of renewable energy in the region. Some nations have maintained consistent shares, while others have shown significant increases or decreases in their renewable energy contributions over time.

Key Insights

  • Laos and Cambodia have significantly increased their renewable energy share
  • Malaysia and Thailand show steady growth in renewable adoption
  • Some countries show fluctuations, indicating policy or economic impacts

GDP & CO₂ Emissions Over Time by Country

Longitudinal Trends Analysis

Longitudinal Trends Analysis

This dual-axis line chart tracks the evolution of both GDP and CO₂ emissions for each ASEAN country over time. The parallel visualization allows for direct comparison of economic and environmental trends.

When both metrics rise in tandem, it suggests carbon-intensive development. Diverging trends may indicate efficiency improvements or transitions to cleaner energy sources. Select different countries from the dropdown to examine national trajectories.

Key Insights

  • Malaysia shows consistent parallel growth in both GDP and emissions
  • The Philippines demonstrates periods of economic growth with stable emissions
  • Most countries experienced a temporary dip in both metrics during 2020
